01 Approach & Philosophy

Performance gaps are
design problems.

"I don't come from L&D theory. I come from performance outcomes."
Patrick Shin, Ph.D.

Most training requests are actually performance problems in disguise. Before building anything, I diagnose: content audits, productivity data, exit interviews, behavioral observation. Then I design the smallest intervention that moves the metric.

Every program I build connects to a business outcome and is designed to run without me. I audit before I build, sunset what underperforms, and distribute ownership so results last. My work is grounded in ADKAR, Kirkpatrick, Bandura's self-efficacy research, and 15 years of testing what actually changes behavior at work.

02 Selected Case Studies
01
Financial Partners Credit Union

AI-Powered Onboarding Redesign

Onboarding · Sales Enablement · AI-Enabled Learning
Role Senior L&D Leader
Scale 300+ employees
Timeline Rebuilt in 30 days
Sector Financial Services

Frontline onboarding took 10 days and staff still weren't performing. Leadership assumed it was a motivation problem. The data said otherwise.

Approach

Diagnosed the gap with content audits and productivity data, then rebuilt onboarding from 10 days to 5. Moved foundational content to self-paced pre-work and used in-person time for simulations and coached practice. Added AI call simulations for NPS recovery, cross-sell, and de-escalation. Built an executive coaching program and impact dashboards for senior leadership.

50% Faster time-to-productivity
30% Reduction in ramp time
10pts NPS improvement, Q1

"Redesigning onboarding isn't about better content. It's about removing everything that doesn't directly accelerate performance on day one."

02
Lakeshore Learning

2,200-Person ERP Change Network

Change Management · ERP Adoption · Train-the-Trainer
Role Change Management PM
Scale 2,200 employees, global
Timeline 90% adoption in 90 days
Sector Retail / Education Products

A global ERP rollout touching 2,200 employees, a fixed go-live date, and a central L&D team that couldn't train everyone.

Approach

Built and certified a 112-person Change Agent network selected from floor-credible high performers. Wrote facilitator guides, required mock facilitation for certification, and set up governance: centralized SharePoint, twice-weekly leadership check-ins, escalation pathways, and unit-level adoption metrics visible to every agent. Produced 300+ training assets for the global workforce.

90% Adoption in 90 days
35% Faster escalation resolution
4.8/5 Training relevance score

"A network without governance is just a group chat. Distributed ownership only holds when people can see their own impact."

03
Biola University

Three-Year Leadership Curriculum

Leadership Development · Retention · Succession
Role Director of L&D
Scale 200+ leaders trained
Timeline 3-year curriculum
Sector Higher Education

Staff tenure averaged one year. First-time leaders got informational workshops and returned to their teams unchanged. 50% turnover was treated as the cost of doing business.

Approach

Designed a three-year leadership curriculum grounded in Bandura's self-efficacy research. Year one built leadership identity, year two applied skills through cohort peer coaching, year three developed the ability to lead others through change. Replaced lectures with blended intensives built into existing team rhythms, and identified emerging leaders one to two years before openings arose.

200% Increase in retention
40% Drop in first-year attrition
70% Leadership retention lift

"A great leadership curriculum doesn't just teach skills. It builds the identity of someone who uses them."

04
Biola University

Building the Basic Needs Center from Zero

Executive Influence · Coalition Building · ADKAR
Role Founder & Director
Scale 30% of student body served
Funding $100K secured
Sector Higher Education

Over a third of college students nationally face food insecurity. Biola had no infrastructure, no budget, and no institutional acknowledgment the problem existed.

Approach

Applied ADKAR at every stage of the change. Presented data and student stories directly to the president and Board of Trustees, reframing food insecurity as a retention and academic success issue. Built a cross-functional coalition across dining, student affairs, and external partners, and secured a $100,000 institutional commitment.

30% Of students served
$100K Institutional commitment secured
2,861 Meals donated, single semester

"The first physical food pantry in the Council for Christian Colleges and Universities — built from a data presentation to a board of trustees."
